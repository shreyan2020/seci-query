INT: Can you describe how you usually decide what to eat?

PART: The main thing I care about is whether the food will taste good. If I expect something to be delicious, that is usually enough for me to choose it. I don't spend much time thinking about calories, nutrition, or health benefits.

INT: So taste is your primary factor?

PART: Yes, definitely. Taste is the most important thing. If something is healthy but doesn't taste good, I probably won't choose it. Eating is one of the main sources of enjoyment in my day, so I don't want it to feel like a chore.

INT: Do you ever consider nutritional value?

PART: Not really. I know some foods are healthier than others, but that usually doesn't influence my decision. I might be aware that something is unhealthy, but if it tastes good, I'll still eat it.

INT: How do you feel after eating something that is considered unhealthy?

PART: Usually satisfied. I rarely regret it. I think enjoyment in the moment is more valuable than worrying about long-term consequences every time I eat.

INT: Do you plan your meals in advance?

PART: No, most of my food decisions are spontaneous. I decide based on what I feel like eating at that moment. If I crave something specific, I'll go out of my way to get it.

INT: What role do cravings play in your decisions?

PART: Cravings are very important. If I crave fried chicken, pizza, or dessert, that becomes my priority. I don't try to suppress cravings. I see them as signals of what I want to experience.

INT: Do you prefer familiar foods or trying new foods?

PART: Both, but the key requirement is that it should be delicious. I'm open to trying new foods, especially if they are known to be flavorful or indulgent.

INT: How do you react to food recommendations that emphasize health?

PART: I'm usually not interested. If someone says something is healthy but doesn't emphasize taste, it doesn't appeal to me. Health alone is not a motivating factor.

INT: Do you ever choose a healthier option when given a choice?

PART: Only if it also tastes very good. Health is not enough on its own to influence my choice. Taste always comes first.

INT: How do you feel about portion sizes?

PART: I don't restrict portion sizes unless I'm physically full. If something tastes great, I'm happy to eat more of it.

INT: Do you associate food with emotions?

PART: Yes. Food is strongly connected to enjoyment, comfort, and reward. Eating something delicious can improve my mood significantly.

INT: Do you feel any obligation to eat healthy?

PART: Not really. I understand that nutrition is important in general, but I don't actively optimize for it. I prefer to focus on enjoying what I eat.

INT: How do you respond to social situations involving food?

PART: Social situations are a great opportunity to eat good food. I tend to choose the most appealing option available rather than the healthiest one.

INT: What do you think about balancing health and taste?

PART: For me, taste takes priority. If something happens to be healthy and delicious, that's good, but I won't sacrifice taste for health.

INT: Do you think your preferences will change in the future?

PART: It's possible, but right now I don't see a reason to change. I value enjoyment, and food is one of the simplest and most reliable ways to experience that.

INT: What motivates your food choices overall?

PART: Maximizing enjoyment. I want food to be something I look forward to, not something I analyze or optimize too much.

INT: Do you track anything related to your eating habits?

PART: No, I don't track calories, macros, or nutrition. I rely entirely on how I feel and what I want.

INT: How do you define a good meal?

PART: A good meal is something that tastes excellent, satisfies my cravings, and makes me feel happy while eating it.

INT: Is there anything else you'd like to add?

PART: Yes. I think food should be enjoyed without overthinking it. For me, the purpose of eating is pleasure, not optimization.
